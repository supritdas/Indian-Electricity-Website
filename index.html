Safety Tips

Your safety is important to us. Here are some useful tips and guidelines on electrical safety to protect yourself, your family, and your property.

  • Hire Professionals: Use licensed electricians for installations and repairs.
  • Inspect Regularly: Check cords, outlets, and switches for damage.
  • Water Safety: Keep electricity away from water, and never use electrical devices with wet hands.
  • GFCIs: Install Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters in wet areas to prevent shocks.
  • Unplug Unused Devices: Save energy and reduce fire risks by unplugging appliances when not in use.
  • Extension Cord Caution: Use them temporarily, avoid hiding them under rugs, and choose the right rating.
  • Avoid Overloading: Don't overload outlets or power strips to prevent overheating.
  • Proper Bulbs: Use bulbs with the correct wattage to avoid overheating.
  • Teach Children Safety: Educate kids about the dangers of playing with electricity.
  • Stay Clear of Power Lines: Never touch or approach power lines.
  • Emergency Preparedness: Know how to turn off power in emergencies and have emergency numbers handy.
